The first term, called the squared bias, represents the extent to which the average prediction over all data sets differs from the desired regression function. The second term, called the variance, measures the extent to which the solutions for individual data sets vary around their average, and hence this measures the extent to which the function y^(xi)\hat{y}(x_i)y^​(xi​)﻿ is sensitive to the particular choice of data set.
